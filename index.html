Simple Refinements

Brian Travelstead


I did not grow up hunting. I grew up cooking.

I have always considered myself lucky to have been raised in a household that cooks. Two, actually. My parents separated at a young age. I grew up eating similar foods in both homes, but the preparation was different. As a teenager, I chose restaurants as my first job. Years went by and what seemed to be an interest born of necessity became both a hobby and a career. As an adult, I have returned to school in hopes of a new career bath but will always carry the passion for food I nurtured for over 20 years.

Hunting came later. I have enjoyed hiking, fishing, and shooting sports most of my life. On many occasions, fishing had brought home higher quality ingrdients than what I could find (read that as afford) at the store. At the suggestion of several of my friends, I tried hunting for the first time in my early thirties. I applied the skills and methods I had picked up in my culiinary education and various kitchens I have worked in to the game I brought home. Venison and squirrel in particular.

Vension, being a red meat simliar to lean beef, has countless prepartation options. Pot roast was one of the meals I mentioned earlier that I loved growing up, whether it was prepared by my mother or my father. For my own home, I prefer to brine the roast in advance. This process is known as "corning". The meat is submerged for several days in a liquid with aromatics and a high salt content. The result is the same flavorful, rose-colored roast served corned beef, but lower in fat and cholesterol.
